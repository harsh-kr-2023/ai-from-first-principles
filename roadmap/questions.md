# Core Research Questions

## Phase 1 — Foundations of Intelligence
### Module 1.1 — The Idea of Intelligence
- What is intelligence and why is it still hard to define?
- Why did early symbolic AI fail on real-world problems?
- What does it mean for an algorithm to reason versus learn?
- How did the notion of search shape the first AI systems?

### Module 1.2 — Learning and Representation
- Why are representations central to intelligence?
- Can intelligence emerge from prediction alone?
- How does generalization differ from memorization?
- Why do some representations make learning easier?

## Phase 2 — Machine Learning
### Module 2.1 — Statistical Learning Fundamentals
- Why does probability matter for decision-making?
- What causes a model to be biased or high-variance?
- When does more data help versus more model capacity?
- How do data quality and representation influence results?

### Module 2.2 — Core Supervised Learning
- Why do linear models remain useful?
- How does loss minimization translate to better predictions?
- Why is evaluation more important than fit?
- When should a simpler model be preferred over a complex one?

## Phase 3 — Deep Learning
### Module 3.1 — Neural Network Foundations
- Why do neural networks need nonlinear activations?
- How does depth affect expressivity and training?
- Why does backpropagation work in practice?
- What makes deep learning different from earlier learning methods?

### Module 3.2 — Training, Regularization, and Architectures
- Why is optimization harder in deep networks?
- How do regularization and normalization improve training?
- Why are convolutional architectures effective for images?
- What does sequence modeling require that other models do not?

## Phase 4 — Language Intelligence
### Module 4.1 — Language as Structure and Meaning
- Why is language fundamentally different from other data?
- Why do embeddings capture meaning?
- How does context change text interpretation?
- Why were early NLP approaches brittle?

### Module 4.2 — Sequence Modeling and Language Tasks
- Why did n-gram models fail on long-range dependencies?
- What problem does recurrence solve in language models?
- Why is generation harder than classification?
- How do we measure quality for language outputs?

## Phase 5 — Transformers
### Module 5.1 — Attention and the Transformer Idea
- Why did attention replace recurrence?
- What does self-attention compute and why is it effective?
- How does the transformer scale better than previous models?
- Why does parallel computation matter for sequence modeling?

### Module 5.2 — Transformer Families and Use Cases
- Why are encoder-only and decoder-only forms different?
- What tradeoffs come with pretraining and fine-tuning?
- How do transformers generalize across modalities?
- When should a transformer be chosen over another architecture?

## Phase 6 — Large Language Models
### Module 6.1 — Scaling and Evaluation
- Why do large language models improve with scale?
- Why are context windows a critical limitation?
- What causes LLM hallucinations?
- Why are evaluation metrics still an open problem?

### Module 6.2 — Responsible LLM Use
- How should prompt engineering be approached responsibly?
- Why does retrieval help large language models?
- What are the risks of deploying LLMs?
- When is an LLM the right tool for a task?
